Spaghetti alla Carbonara

Carbonara, a Roman dish made with eggs, hard cheese, and cured pork. Its rich and silky sauce comes from beaten eggs tossed with hot pasta. The trick to making delicious carbonara is to make sure the pasta is hot enough to cook the eggs, but not so hot that they curdle.

Ingredients:

  • Oil: Olive Oil reccommended!
  • Guanciale: If you canâ€™t find it, you can substitute unsmoked bacon or pancetta.
  • Spaghetti: Use store-bought or homemade spaghetti noodles. If you like, you can substitute bucatini for the spaghetti
  • Eggs: 3 Large Eggs(add richness and flavor)
  • Cheese: Go for hand-shredded Pecorino Romano cheese. Substitutes- Parmesan, if preferred!
  • Seasoning: Carbonara goes well with Salt and Pepper.

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Cook the pork in olive oil until browned and crispy, then drain on paper towels.
  2. Boil the spaghetti in salted water. Drain and return to the pot. Let cool.
  3. Whisk the eggs, 1/2 of the cheese, and some pepper in a bowl until smooth.
  4. Pour the egg mixture over the pasta, stirring quickly, until creamy.
  5. Stir in the pork, then top with the remaining cheese and more black pepper.
